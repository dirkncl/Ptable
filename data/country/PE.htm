Peru
Calcium
Peru's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) in 2006 was 394289 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2006 was 142.5 millions tonnes.
Iron
Peru's production of iron ore in 2006 was 5.885 million tonnes. Total world production of iron ore in 2006 was 1.81 billion tonnes.
Production of pig iron in Peru in 2006 was 306000 tonnes. Total world production of pig iron in 2006 was 925.8 million tonnes.
Copper
In 2006, Peru's production of copper by mining was 1.049 million tonnes, total world production was 15.1 million tonnes.
In 2006, Peru produced 507710 tonnes of refined copper, total production of refined copper was 17.2 million tonnes.
Zinc
In 2006, Peru was the third largest producer of zinc, with around 1.2 million tonnes, about 11% of world total.
Arsenic
In 2006, Peru produced 4399 tonnes of white arsenic (As2O3). Peru's production comes mostly from copper mining.
Selenium
In 2006, Peru produced 75 tonnes of selenium.
Silver
In 2007, Peru was the world's top producer of silver, the Uchucchacua mine being one of the top silver-producing mines in the world. Peru has been mining silver since 1546.
Cadmium
In 2006, Peru produced 416 tonnes of cadmium, world production was around 21100 tonnes.
Tin
Peru's mine production of tin in 2006 was 38470 tonnes, making it the world's third largest producer. World production in 2006 was 321 thousand tonnes.
Peru's smelter production of tin in 2006 was 40495 tonnes, making it the world's third largest producer. World smelter production in 2006 was 340 thousand tonnes.
Tellurium
In 2006, Peru was the world's second largest producer of tellurium with 37 tonnes, world production was around 122 tonnes.
Barium
In 2006, Peri produced 1899 tonnes of barytes (BaSO4), the main source of barium. World production of barytes in 2006 was 8.8 million tonnes.
Gold
In 2006, Peru's mine production of gold was 203.27 tonnes. Total amount of gold mined in 2006 was 2310 tonnes.
Mercury
More than 100,000 tons of mercury were mined from the region of Huancavelica, Peru, over the course of three centuries following the discovery of deposits there in 1563. The patio process and later pan amalgamation process continued to create great demand for mercury to treat silver ores until the late 1800s.
Lead

In 2006, Peru's production of refined lead was 120311 tonnes. Total world production of refined lead in 2006 was 8 million tonnes.
Bismuth
In 2006, Peru produced 1081 tonnes of bismuth, total world production of bismuth in 2006 was around 5200 tonnes.
