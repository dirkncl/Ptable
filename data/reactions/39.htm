Reactions of Yttrium
Reactions with water
When finely divided, or heated, yttrium metal dissolves in water to form solutions containing the aquated Y(III) ion together with hydrogen gas.
  2Y(s) + 6H2O(aq)2Y3+(aq) + 6OH-(aq) + 3H2(g)
Reactions with air
Yttrium tarnishes slowly in air and burns readily to form yttrium (III) oxide.
  4Y(s) + 3O2(g)2Y2O3(s)
Reactions with halogens
Yttrium is very reactive towards the halogens and burns to form yttrium(III) trihalides.
  2Y(s) + 3F2(g)2YF3(s)
  2Y(s) + 3Cl2(g)2YCl3(s)
  2Y(s) + 3Br2(g)2YBr3(s)
  2Y(s) + 3I2(g)2YI3(s)
Reactions with acids
Yttrium dissolves readily in dilute hydrochloric acid to form solutions containing the aquated Y(III) ion together with hydrogen gas.
  2Y(s) + 6HCl(aq)2Y3+(aq) + 6Cl-(aq) + 3H2(g)
